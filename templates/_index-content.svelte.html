The map below shows how Minnesota voted by precinct, which are the smallest units of geography election counters use to to tabulate votes — often comparable in size to a neighborhood or a small town.

The map is colored based on vote density. Darker areas represent smaller, more densely populated precincts — like cities — where lighter areas represent larger, less populous precincts, like rural areas and small towns.
